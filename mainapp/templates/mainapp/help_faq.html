Help & FAQ

Everything you need to know about SkillSwap

How SkillSwap Works

1. Sign Up

Create your free account and get 2 hours of time credits as a welcome bonus

2. Offer Services

Share your skills and earn time credits by helping others

3. Exchange

Use your earned credits to request services from others

Key Principle: One hour of time = One time credit, regardless of the service. Everyone's time is valued equally in our community!

Frequently Asked Questions

A time bank is a reciprocity-based work trading system where everyone's time is valued equally. Members exchange services using time as currency. One hour of work = one time credit, regardless of what service is provided.

You can earn time credits by:

  • Signing up (2 hours welcome bonus)
  • Daily login (2 hours per day)
  • Offering services to other members
  • Completing service requests

To request a service:

  1. Browse available services or search by category
  2. Click on a service you're interested in
  3. Enter the number of hours you need
  4. Submit your request and wait for acceptance

You can offer virtually any skill or service! Common categories include:

  • Teaching & Tutoring (languages, music, academics)
  • Tech Support (computer repair, web design)
  • Home Services (gardening, cleaning, repairs)
  • Professional Services (writing, design, consulting)
  • Creative Services (photography, art, crafts)
  • And many more!

No, time credits cannot be converted to real money. SkillSwap operates on a pure time-exchange basis. The value is in the services you receive and the community connections you build, not monetary gain.

You can always earn more time credits by offering your services to others! Additionally, you receive 2 time credits for logging in daily. The community thrives on reciprocity - give your time to receive time.

After a service is completed and accepted, you can leave a review for the service provider. Reviews help build trust in the community and recognize quality service. You can rate from 1-5 stars and leave an optional comment.
